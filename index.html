About
I don't just design screens.
I interpret problems — and build
the case for the right solution.

As a UX designer at Cognizant, I work at the intersection of user needs, business constraints, and technical reality. My approach starts from research — not assumptions — and the empathy maps, journey analyses, and stakeholder sessions I run aren't process for process's sake. They're the work that gets the right ideas greenlit.

Over the past years I've led the UX direction for AI-assisted feedback tools, internal data platforms, VR research environments, and medical portals. The common thread: making complex systems feel simple, without hiding the complexity that matters.

I push back when needed. When an AI-powered direction was shelved for timeline reasons on one project, I used the research data to build the case — and got it back on the roadmap. Good design sometimes means holding your ground with evidence.

How I work
Design is a decision-making
process — not a deliverable.

Every project I touch follows the same underlying logic: understand the real problem before solving it, make the invisible visible, and build the case with evidence.

01
Discover & Listen
Stakeholder interviews, user research, empathy mapping. I don't assume — I ask. Then I synthesise what I find into clear pain points and opportunity areas that the whole team can align on.
02
Define & Frame
Problem statements, How Might We's, information architecture. This is where I push back on scope creep, make the case for the right direction, and establish what success actually looks like.
03
Design & Prototype
Wireframes, flows, high-fidelity screens, interactive prototypes. I design with constraints in mind — technical, timeline, and business — so that what I hand off can actually be built.
04
Test & Measure
Usability testing, A/B validation, post-launch tracking. Good design should be measurable. I track what changes and use results to drive the next iteration — not just declare success.
